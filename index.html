General Information

Welcome to the MiCCrobioTAckle Workshop on Advanced Programming in Research Techniques.

This workshop is a key component of the MiCCrobioTAckle initiative, a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Doctoral Network dedicated to pioneering research at the intersection of gut microbiota and cancer cachexia. Our mission is to train the next generation of leaders in microbiota medicine, fostering innovative approaches to disease treatment through personalized, targeted, and preventive healthcare strategies.

We’re not just decoding bacteria—we’re decoding the future of medicine.

This workshop is designed to equip participants with fundamental skills in Programming and Data Analysis, essential for modern biomedical research. We introduce an efficient and reproducible workflow in R for data analysis. Through hands-on training in R, attendees will learn to implement efficient and reproducible workflows, utilizing various software tools that are basically the Swiss army knife of data analysis. The curriculum is tailored for individuals with minimal to no prior computational experience, including doctors, researchers, and students, building upon their existing domain knowledge to facilitate immediate application in their respective fields. Participants will be encouraged to collaborate and apply the acquired skills to their own research challenges, fostering a supportive learning environment that promotes the integration of computational techniques into biomedical research. We are excited to embark on this educational journey with you. By the end of this course, you’ll be able to write clean, reproducible code, produce customized figures, and leave with a mini R toolkit tailored to your lab life – and the confidence to explore more on your own.
