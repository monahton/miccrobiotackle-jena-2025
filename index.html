General Information

In MiCCrobioTAckle , we aim to Advancing Research at the Intersection Between Gut Microbiota and Cancer Cachexia to Train Europe’s Future Leaders in Microbiota Medicine. Our project offers a unique opportunity to advance microbiota medicine as a new clinical approach to treat diseases and offer personalized, targeted, and preventive approaches to healthcare. We strive to deliver microbiome-based solutions to improve clinical management of cancer cachexia. At MiCCrobioTAckle, 12 doctoral candidates will participate in an intensive training-through-research program across disciplines and sectors. With academic and industrial partners in the fields of computational biology, microbiology, synthetic biology, clinical, and experimental science, our consortium is prepared to train the Europe’s future leader in microbiota medicine.

This workshop aims to develop and teach fundamental skills needed for advanced Programming in Research techniques through hands-on training. We introduce an efficient and reproducible workflow in R for using various softwares and tools for data analysis. This workshop target audience include doctors/researchers/students who have little to no prior computational experience, and its lessons are domain specific, building on learners' existing knowledge to enable them to quickly apply skills learned to their own work/research.
Participants will be encouraged to help one another and to apply what they have learned to their own research problems.
